About the Park

Prince Edward Island National Park was established in 1937 and covers about 21.5 km2 of land over a 40k stretch of the coast of Prince Edward Island. It is recognized for its red sand beaches, sand dunes, and sandstone cliffs.

Through looking at all of the maps from each park (and I've been writing each page in order) I am starting to understand the design style that exists today. I see what they're trying to do I just don't like it. I just happen to think that maps are a place where the designs matter less than the maps themselves, by which I mean you can do essentially anything to a map while designing it (granted all of the elements of the map itself are accurate) and still produce a map that can be read and understood. The sand could be purple and we would know what it is. So why is it beige. It doesn't have to be beige.
